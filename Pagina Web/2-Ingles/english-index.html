Lavaderos de la Reina's stands out as a legendary destination within the Sierra Nevada mountains. Situated in the profound glacial cirque beneath the Picón de Jerez, the Puntal de los Cuartos, and Tajos Negros, at an elevation of 2500 meters, the arrival of thaw brings forth a place of remarkable beauty. Water courses through the rocks, shaping the Covatillas stream and the springs of the Maitena river, whose cascading waters form numerous waterfalls and streams. These natural features are enveloped by high-altitude pastures dotted with snow stars, crafting a breathtaking spectacle. Embarking on this route is essential for those seeking to behold the Sierra Nevada landscapes in their full magnificence. Additionally, the path traverses the Loma de los Cuartos, offering unparalleled vistas of Veleta, Mulhacén, Alcazaba, and Cerro de los Machos.

The allure of its beauty emanates from its distinctive natural characteristics. An ancient cirque of glacial descent adorned with towering mountain peaks, snow-covered slopes, expansive snowfields, verdant meadows, and serene lagoons shapes this extraordinary terrain, reminiscent of regions further north rather than Andalusia. Additionally, the dark soil blanketing its rocky facade, teeming with vibrant green lichens and algae during the thawing period, further enhances its appeal. Thus, it becomes clear why the Lavaderos de la Reina stands as one of Sierra Nevada's premier routes.

History

Las Covatillas is the real name of the Lavaderos, but Queen Fabiola of Belgium fell in love with the place on several visits. It is said that on one of these occasions, when she was told that the springs were locally called lavaderos, she exclaimed: "If a queen came to wash here, she would gain in nobility". This may not be true, but the beauty of the place is well worth it.
